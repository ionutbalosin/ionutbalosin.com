# Software Architecture Essentials — Designing for the AI Era

## Description

Equip your architects and senior engineers to design systems that survive contact with reality — including the new reality of AI as both a tool that supports the architect and a component that runs inside the systems we design. The course goes from architectural fundamentals (quality attributes, tactics, views, ADRs, evaluation) to architecting in the AI era (agentic patterns, fitness functions, cost, non-determinism, prompt injection).

**What the course covers**

- The main concepts and context of software architecture — essential requirements identification, architectural design, architectural documentation, and architecture analysis.
- A clear understanding of what it means to be a software architect, as well as the different types of architects (e.g. enterprise, solution, software, and system).
- A structured way of collecting and documenting business requirements with an architectural impact (i.e. non-functional requirements) so they are quantifiable, measurable, and testable.
- Design techniques driven by Quality Attributes (availability, performance, scalability, security, modifiability, testability, usability).
- How to create proper and structured architectural documentation aligned to its purpose and beneficiaries (i.e. its users).
- Techniques to evaluate software architecture and mitigate architectural risk before it becomes an incident.
- A practical recipe to evaluate competing frameworks and libraries and pick the most suitable for the project's context.
- How the architect's role evolves in the AI era — AI both as a tool that supports the architect (gap analysis, fitness functions, diagrams from code) and as a component that runs inside the systems we design (non-determinism, cost, hallucinations, prompt injection, agentic patterns).
- A blend of lectures, individual and team exercises, and interactive workshops.

## What You Will Learn

By the end of this course, your architects and senior engineers will be able to:

- Translate business goals and stakeholder concerns into measurable Architecturally Significant Requirements (ASRs).
- Apply proven architectural tactics for availability, performance, scalability, security, modifiability, testability, and usability.
- Document architecture for the right audience using established frameworks (Kruchten 4+1, C4, Rozanski & Woods) and a *docs-as-code* approach.
- Write Architectural Decision Records that act as active guardrails — including for AI-assisted development.
- Evaluate competing technologies and frameworks with a structured evaluation matrix instead of preference and hype.
- Design systems that embed AI components, explicitly accounting for non-determinism, cost, hallucinations, prompt injection, and agentic patterns.
- Evaluate an existing architecture and surface architectural risk early, not after the incident.
